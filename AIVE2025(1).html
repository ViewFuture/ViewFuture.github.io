About AIVE 2025

Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies, including machine learning (ML), natural language processing (NLP), and large-scale data analytics, are revolutionizing education by providing learners and educators with a more innovative, flexible, and personalized educational environment to enhance teaching and learning experiences. With the exponential growth of online learning platforms and digital content, educators face the challenges of effectively utilizing vast educational resources and recommending tailored materials to individual learners. Demonstrating significant capabilities in data analysis, pattern recognition, and adaptive learning, AI technologies are garnering substantial interest and demand, particularly for assisting in personalized tutoring, content creation, and the development of intelligent learning systems. The integration of AI technologies enhances education by making it more engaging, accessible, and customized to the specific needs of individual learners, while also breaking down language barriers. As such, this workshop aims to bring together researchers interested in AI applications across all aspects of technology-enhanced education, facilitating collaboration among educators, linguistic experts, data specialists, and AI researchers. We hope that the application of AI in education can improve the learning experience, leading to greater effectiveness, efficiency, and personalization in education. The International Workshop on Artificial Intelligence in Vocational Education (AIVE 2025) aims to provide a platform for researchers to exchange the latest advancements in theories, datasets, methods, metrics, applications, and more, fostering the development of this interdisciplinary field.

Workshop topics of interest include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • • AI and its Applications in Vocational Education
  • • Large Models / Foundation Models for Vocational Education
  • • Development and Implementation of Multimodal Models in Vocational Education
  • • AI Generated Content (AIGC) for Vocational Education
  • • Real-World Impact and Effectiveness of AI Models in Vocational Education
  • • Quality Assessment of Content Generation in Vocational Education
  • • Non-Reference/Reference-based Metrics for AI Models in Vocational Education
  • • Language Translation and Natural Language Understanding in Vocational Education
  • • Natural Language Processing in Vocational Education
  • • Big Data in Technology-Enhanced Learning
  • • Content Recommendation for Vocational Education Applications
  • • Text Simplification and Summarization for Digital Vocational Education Contents
  • • Data Mining on Digital Vocational Education Resources
  • • Automatic Question Answering for Assisting Vocational Education
  • • Computational Linguistics for Vocational Education Data Processing and Evaluation
  • • Interpretation of GAI Model Outputs for Vocational Education
  • • Digital Libraries and Corpora for Vocational Education

Paper Submission

All submissions must be in PDF format. Authors should avoid the use of non-English fonts to prevent problems with printing and viewing. All accepted papers will be published by Springer. Please follow strictly the instructions for LNCS authors. Springer's LNCS site offers style files and information. Format for the submission are either Long Paper (12-15 pages) or Short Paper (6-11 pages), including main content and references. Extra pages will have a cost of 100 AUD to Springer German publication.

Submissions MUST not have been published previously, and must not be under review for publication while being considered for GAIE. This applies also to papers with significant overlapping contributions. Authors are advised to interpret these limitations strictly and to contact the workshop chairs in case of doubt.

Submissions MUST follow LNCS (Lecture Notes in Computer Science) format. We encourage authors to cite related work comprehensively, and when citing conference papers please also consider to cite their extended journal versions if applicable.

Travel Information

Public Transportation:

You can take Metro. As of Dec. 2021, Shanghai Metro had 20 lines in operation (including maglev lines), with 508 stations (including 2 stations on maglev lines), and a total length of 831 km (including 29 km of maglev lines). The statistics do not include Jinshan Railway. Shanghai also has the world's most extensive bus system with nearly one thousand bus lines, operated by numerous transportation companies. Not all of Shanghai's bus routes are numbered - some have names exclusively in Chinese. Bus fares are usually ¥1, ¥1.5 or ¥2, sometimes higher, while Metro fares run from ¥3 to ¥14 depending on distance. To learn more, visit the Metro & Maglev Train page.

Taxi:

Taxis in Shanghai are plentiful and market competition has driven taxi fare down to affordable prices for the average resident (¥14（¥18 after 11pm）for the first 3 kilometers). Taking a taxi is a fast and convenient way to get around Shanghai. The city has the best-managed taxi service in China. To learn more, visit the Shanghai Taxis page.
